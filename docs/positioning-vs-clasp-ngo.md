# Distinguishing CLASP-the-protocol from CLASP-the-NGO

## The short version

CLASP, as used in this project, refers to the **Clause-Level Access and Standards Protocol**, an open specification (CC-BY-4.0) for licensed AI-system access to engineering and technical standards content. It is unrelated to CLASP, the **Collaborative Labeling and Appliance Standards Program**, an international NGO that has operated in the appliance energy-efficiency standards space since 1999. The two projects share an acronym only; they have no affiliation, lineage, or shared personnel.

## What each project is

| | Clause-Level Access and Standards Protocol | Collaborative Labeling and Appliance Standards Program |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Domain | Engineering and technical standards (process automation, software, safety, regulatory) | Residential and commercial appliance energy efficiency |
| Output | A wire-level specification and a turnkey gateway platform (Norma) | Policy guidance, research, country-level program implementation support |
| Audience | Engineering SDOs, AI-vendor implementers, AI-assisted-engineering tooling users | Energy ministries, utilities, manufacturers, retailers |
| Web | (clasp.sevres.org, once registered) | clasp.ngo |
| First active | 2026 | 1999 |
| Built on | IAB Tech Lab's CoMP v1.0; MCP | n/a |

## Why the names happen to coincide

"CLASP" reads naturally as a verb in the standards space (a clasp fastens, holds together). Both projects landed on it independently, and the acronyms spell out to different things. Neither is a registered trademark. USPTO TESS shows two LIVE word-mark registrations for "CLASP" in adjacent classes (pediatric healthcare, oncology pharmaceuticals), neither in conflict with either project.

This is the same situation as Apache HTTP (the web server) and Apache (the helicopter), or Mercury (the planet, element, automotive brand, messaging library, and Roman god). Acronym collisions are common; legal and reputational coexistence is the norm.

## Practical guidance for outreach material

1. **First use, always spell it out:** "Clause-Level Access and Standards Protocol (CLASP)."
2. **First mention in any document directed at a non-engineering-software audience should include the one-line disclaimer above**, because the appliance-standards NGO has 25 years of search-engine head start.
3. **Use the spec domain** (e.g., claspspec.org) rather than a bare "clasp" URL; this gives the audience a direct cue that this is the protocol, not the NGO.
4. **Don't reference the NGO uncharitably.** They are a legitimate organization doing useful work in their domain. The goal is clarity, not contrast.

For an FAQ, footer, or hover-text:

It is unrelated to CLASP, the Collaborative Labeling and Appliance Standards Program (clasp.ngo), which works on appliance energy-efficiency standards.
